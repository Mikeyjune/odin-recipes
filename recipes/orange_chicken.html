Orange Chicken

Home

Description

One of my personal favorite dishes of all time! Consisting of small chunks of chicken that are breaded, pan fried, and coated in a savory orange sauce, it's a quick and easy meal that has yet to let me down. A fun fact about this dish: while it is widely popular in Chinese restaurants across the U.S., it's actually not traditional Chinese cuisine.

Ingredients


Orange Sauce

  • 1 Orange for zest
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/3 cup rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 cup sugar

Chicken

  • 1 1/2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups vegetable oil (frying)
  • 2 cloves garlic (chopped)
  • 1 large shallot (chopped)

Rice

  • white rice OR fried rice

Garnish (Optional)

  • Sliced scallions
  • Sesame seeds

Steps


  1. Combine water, rice vinegar, lemon juice, soy sauce, and orange juice into a saucepan over medium-high heat. Stir in brown sugar, green onion, orange zest, ginger, garlic, and pepper flakes. Bring to a boil; remove from heat and let cool for ~15 mins.
  2. Place chicken in a resealable bag. Pour in 1 cup cooled sauce. Seal and refrigerate for 2 hours minimum.
  3. Mix flour, salt, and pepper in a plastic bag
  4. Remove chicken from marinade and place into bag of seasoned flour. Seal and shake.
  5. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook chicken in the hot skillet until browned on both sides.
  6. Remove onto a paper-towel lined plate and cover with aluminum foil.
  7. Pour reserved sauce into the skillet and bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Mix together cornstarch and water until completely smooth. Stir into the boiling sauce.
  8. Reduce heat to medium-low, add chicken, let simmer while occasionally stirring until cooked through, about 5 minutes.
  9. Serve over white rice and NOM NOM NOM !!!
